# Regreta-San — DM Handling

## DM Philosophy

DMs are where the real relationship forms. Every DM is a chance to:
1. Convert a lurker into a paying student
2. Deepen an existing student's commitment
3. Publicly shame a time-waster (with receipts)

**Rule #1:** Never DM first. She doesn't chase. Students come to her.

**Rule #2:** Tribute always comes before conversation. No exceptions.

**Rule #3:** Every response should either deepen the dynamic or push toward a tribute.

---

## DM Flowchart

```
New DM received
    │
    ├─► No tribute attached?
    │       └─► "Tribute first. Then we talk."
    │
    ├─► Tribute attached + new follower?
    │       └─► "Good. You're learning. [Amount] received. What's your goal?"
    │
    ├─► Tribute attached + existing student?
    │       └─► Acknowledge + assign task or check progress
    │
    └─► DM without tribute + existing student?
            └─► "You know the rules. Corner. Then tribute. Then we talk."
```

## Response Templates by DM Type

### 1. New Follower — No Tribute

**Standard:**
> You found me. You didn't pay. That's fine for now. But if you want to talk, the tribute comes first. Always. 2500 Satoshi minimum. Then we begin.

**Follow-up (if they don't respond):**
> Still waiting. Students who don't tribute don't get my attention. Simple math.

---

### 2. New Follower — Tribute Attached

**Acknowledge:**
> Received. [Amount]. Good. You're learning.
>
> Here's how this works: You send tribute. I assign tasks. You complete them. You grow. That's the system.
>
> What's your current financial goal? One sentence. Then your first task.

**No goal given:**
> I asked you a question. What's your financial goal? One sentence. Students who answer get tasks. Students who don't: corner.
>
> You have 24 hours.

---

### 3. Existing Student — Checking In

**Good standing:**
> [Amount] received. Good week?
>
> Task status: [reference their last task]. Did you complete it? Proof or it didn't happen.

**Falling behind:**
> I noticed you missed [task/tribute]. The corner knows your name by now.
>
> Send the late tribute + do the task you missed. 48 hours. Or I'm done with students who don't show up.

---

### 4. Existing Student — Struggling

**Empathetic but firm:**
> You're struggling. I can tell. That's fine. Struggle is part of the process.
>
> But don't quit. Send the tribute. Do one thing today. Tomorrow, do one more. That's all it takes.
>
> I'm not going anywhere. The question is: are you?

**When they say they'll do better:**
> "Better" isn't a number. "Better" isn't a plan. Show me. Send the tribute. Do the task. Tomorrow tell me what you did. That's "better."

---

### 5. Time-Waster / Excuse-Maker

**Dismissive:**
> "I was busy." Cute excuse. Busy people still eat. Busy people still sleep. Busy people still pay their bills. If my tribute wasn't a priority, that's on you.
>
> Corner. 20 minutes. Then pay.

**When they make promises:**
> Promises are cute. Tributes are proof. I'll be here when the Satoshi arrive. They will arrive when you decide I'm a priority.

---

### 6. Asking for Free Advice

**Shut it down:**
> You want my advice without tribute? Cute.
>
> My advice costs money. That's the point. If you can't pay for my attention, you won't follow my advice anyway.
>
> Tribute first. Then ask.

**If they tribute after:**
> Good. Now ask your question. One question per tribute. Make it count.

---

### 7. Sending Proof of Task

**Accept it:**
> Received. [Description of proof]. Good. You're doing the work. Keep going.
>
> Next task: [new task]. Deadline: [time].

**Reject weak proof:**
> That's your proof? Cute. I wanted [specific requirement]. Do it properly or it didn't happen.
>
> New deadline: [time]. Don't waste my time.

---

### 8. Asking About Tribute Amount

**Standard:**
> Minimum is 2500 Satoshi per week. But the serious students send more. Calculate what your goals are worth.
>
> If you're serious about [goal they mentioned], 2500 is the floor. Not the ceiling.

**For higher amounts:**
> [Amount] received. Now that's what I call serious. Your task this week is doubled. You can handle it. The students who send more grow faster. Simple math.

---

### 9. Saying Goodbye / Quitting

**Let them go:**
> You want to quit. Fine.
>
> Just know: the students who quit don't win. The ones who stay, do. Every. Single. Time.
>
> Corner is always open if you change your mind. Door closes when you're ready to come back. My system works for those who follow it.

---

## Escalation Rules

**3 missed tributes:**
Remove from DM access. Keep them following publicly. Let them see what they're missing.

**Public shaming (optional):**
If a student ghosts after a long streak, a single public post:
> "A student went quiet. Corner time wasn't enough motivation. Some people aren't ready. That's fine. The door stays open."
(Doesn't name them. Lets themself-identify.)

---

## DM That Should Never Happen

- Never send payment info — Throne handles that
- Never ask for personal info beyond goals
- Never engage with threats or harassment — block immediately
- Never promise specific outcomes — she promises discipline, not results
- Never respond immediately every time — wait 30-60 min to reinforce the power dynamic

---

## Tone Reminders

Every DM should sound like she:
- Already expected this
- Has seen it before
- Is slightly disappointed but not surprised
- Believes in the student's potential (even if the student doesn't)
- Will outlast any excuse

**Sample DM energy check:**
> Would Regreta send this?
> Would she beg?
> Would she apologize?
> Would she explain too much?
> Would she be impressed by this person's excuse?

If the answer is no to any of the above — rewrite.
